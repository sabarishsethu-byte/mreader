Methodology

Our 3 Trading Strategies Explained

Understanding our methodology helps you execute signals with full confidence and context. Each strategy serves a different market condition.

Price Action Trading

Pure ChartsNo IndicatorsMulti-Timeframe

Price Action reads raw candlestick data across M15, H1, H4, and D1 — no lagging indicators, no noise. Just clean structure. Benefit to you: You learn to read any market, any timeframe, independently.

How it benefits signals: Price Action confirmation means entries are placed at the most precise, lowest-risk points on the chart — maximising reward while minimising stop distance.

Candlestick Patterns

Engulfing candles, pin bars, inside bars at key zones. Only traded at confluence — never in isolation. Each pattern signals institutional interest at that price.

Support & Resistance

Multi-touch horizontal levels, dynamic trendlines, round numbers. Levels with 3+ touches carry the highest probability for institutional reaction.

Trend Structure

Higher highs/higher lows = bullish. Lower highs/lower lows = bearish. We only trade with the dominant structure — never against the tide.

Risk-Reward Discipline

Minimum 1:2 R:R on every signal. Entries refined to nearest structure to shrink stop distance and maximise profit potential.

Smart Money Concepts

Institutional FootprintsLiquidityOrder Blocks

SMC decodes how banks and hedge funds move price. We trade alongside institutions, not against them. Benefit to you: You stop getting stop-hunted and start understanding why "false breakouts" happen — and how to profit from them.

How it benefits signals: By entering at institutional order blocks instead of retail support/resistance, our entries are far more precise with smaller stops and larger targets.

Order Blocks

The last bearish candle before a bullish impulse — where institutions re-entered longs. Price often returns here precisely to fuel the next leg up.

Liquidity Sweeps

Price is engineered to trigger retail stop-losses (equal highs/lows) before reversing. We enter AFTER the sweep — when the manipulation is confirmed complete.

Break of Structure (BOS)

Confirmed structural shift signals a trend change. Used as our primary entry filter — we only trade new directions after BOS is confirmed, not anticipated.

Fair Value Gaps (FVG)

3-candle imbalances created by aggressive institutional moves. Price frequently returns to fill these gaps — providing extremely precise, low-risk entry zones.

ICT Methodology

Time-BasedKill ZonesAlgorithmic

ICT (Inner Circle Trader) aligns entries with the exact time windows when institutional order flow is highest. Benefit to you: You stop trading at random times and start understanding that the best moves happen in specific, predictable windows.

How it benefits signals: Kill Zone timing means our entries are placed during maximum liquidity — tighter spreads, faster execution, and larger institutional participation driving price toward our targets.

Kill Zones

London (02:00–05:00 EST) and New York (07:00–10:00 EST) sessions — the highest-probability windows. We issue signals aligned with these windows specifically.

Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)

62%–79% Fibonacci retracement of a swing after a confirmed displacement move. The mathematically precise entry zone used by algorithmic institutional systems.

Power of 3 (AMD)

Accumulation → Manipulation → Distribution. After manipulation (stop hunt) is confirmed, we enter the Distribution phase — the highest R:R portion of any institutional move.

Premium & Discount

Above 50% of a swing = sell zone. Below 50% = buy zone. Aligns your bias with institutional preference — you only buy cheap and sell expensive in structural terms.
